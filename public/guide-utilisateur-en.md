## 1. Getting started

### Create an account

1. Click **Sign in** in the top right corner
2. Click **Sign up**
3. Fill in:
   - **Display name**: your name as it will appear on the platform (minimum 2 characters)
   - **Email**: your email address
   - **Password**: minimum 6 characters
   - **Verification question**: a simple question for NLP practitioners
4. Click **Create account**
5. **Check your email**: a confirmation link will be sent to you. Your account will only be active after clicking this link.

**Note**: only the home page is accessible without signing in. Access to the library, community, resources and all other pages requires an account.

### Sign in

1. Click **Sign in**
2. Enter your email and password
3. Click **Sign in**

### Forgot password

1. On the sign-in page, click **Forgot password?**
2. Enter your email
3. Click **Send link**
4. Check your inbox and follow the reset link

## 2. Navigation

The navigation bar at the top of the screen provides access to all sections:

| Link | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Home** | News feed, statistics and getting started guide |
| **Library** | Browse and search NLP models |
| **Events** | View and register for events |
| **Contribute** | Propose a new model |
| **Community** | Discussion forum |
| **Resources** | NLP glossary, guide and quality criteria |
| **Contributors** | The practitioners who bring this Lab to life |
| **Help** | In-app user guide |

At the bottom of each page, the **footer** displays the application version, a link to atelierpnl.eu and a link to support the Lab.

### Theme

A button in the header (sun/moon/palette icon) lets you choose from 4 themes:

| Theme | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Classic** | Blue-grey and teal — clean and professional (default) |
| **Dark** | Dark mode with bright teal accents |
| **Vivid** | Purple, pink and teal — modern and colorful |
| **Vivid dark** | Vivid in dark mode |

Your choice is saved automatically and persists between visits.

### Mobile installation (PWA)

The Lab can be installed as an app on your phone:

1. Open **lab.atelierpnl.eu** in Safari (iPhone) or Chrome (Android)
2. Tap the **Share** button (Safari) or the **three-dot menu** (Chrome)
3. Choose **"Add to Home Screen"** (Safari) or **"Install app"** (Chrome)
4. The app will appear on your home screen with the Lab icon

The app runs in full screen and previously visited pages remain accessible even without an internet connection.

On mobile, the navigation collapses into a hamburger menu.

## 3. Home

The home page is the entry point to the platform.

### Statistics

Three cards display in real time:
- **Models**: total number of approved models
- **Published**: models with "Published" status
- **Contributors**: number of unique contributors

### New to the Lab?

A welcome block guides new visitors with four links:
- **Explore the library** — browse approaches, tools and experiences
- **Read the resources** — guides, NLP glossary and methodology
- **Join the community** — connect with practitioners
- **Support the project** — help keep the Lab open and free

### Announcement

If an administrator has published an announcement, it appears in a colored banner between the hero and the statistics.

### Latest news

A chronological news feed displays recent platform activity:
- **New model** (green badge) — a model has just been added
- **Update** (colored badge) — an evolution journal entry with version and contributors
- **Resource** (blue badge) — a new article has been published in Resources

Each item is clickable and leads directly to the relevant sheet or resource.

## 4. Model Library

The library is organized into **three tabs**:

### Catalogue tab

Main view of models, organized into three sections:
1. **Approaches** — coherent architectures grouping several models around a vision
2. **Tools** — protocols, formats and techniques usable in sessions
3. **Experiences** — maps of lived experiences, problem or resource states

#### Search and filter

- **Search bar**: filter by title or tag
- **Filter by type**: All types / Experience / Tool / Approach
- **Filter by status**: All statuses / Draft / Under review / Testing / Published / Evolving

### Pipeline tab

A **Kanban** board with 5 columns shows the progression of all models through the lifecycle:

| Column | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| Draft | Model being written |
| Under review | Submitted for peer review |
| Testing | In validation phase |
| Published | Validated and accessible to all |
| Evolving | Published but being improved |

### By approach tab

A tree view for browsing models by approach:
- Each **approach** is a collapsible block (click to open/close)
- Inside: the **tools** and **experiences** linked to that approach
- A **"View sheet"** link provides direct access to the approach sheet
- At the bottom: a **"No approach"** section groups models not yet linked to an approach

## 5. Model details

The model detail page is organized into tabs.

### Header

Displays the title, description, badges (type, status, version), tags, author (clickable to view their profile), associated approach and counters (views, variants, discussions).

For **Approach** type models, a block lists all tools and experiences linked to that approach.

If you are the model's author, an **Edit** button appears to edit the fields.

### Presentation tab

Displays the model's sections with **Markdown rendering**. When a model has 3 or more sections, they are organized into **clickable sub-tabs** for easier reading.

The available sections depend on the type:

| Type | Sections |
|------|----------|
| **Tool** | Protocol, Active principle, Points of vigilance, Known variants, Prerequisites |
| **Experience** | Patterns, Recognizable signals, Intervention points, Prerequisites |
| **Approach** | Philosophy, Creators, Toolkit, Prerequisites |

### Links tab

Displays external links and resources (videos, documents, trainings) as clickable cards.

### History tab

- **Contributors** — list of all contributors, calculated from the evolution journal
- **Evolution journal** — history of changes with version, date, contributors and description
- **Derived models** — variants created from this model

### Variants tab

Displays **derived models** — complete sheets that adapt or enrich the model for a particular case. You can create a variant via the **"Create a variant"** button.

### Discussions tab

Groups **linked forum posts** for this model and existing **feedback**. The **"Give feedback"** button opens the forum with a post pre-linked to the model, allowing the entire community to participate in the discussion.

## 6. Contributing a model

Accessible via the **Contribute** menu (requires being signed in).

### Form

| Field | Description | Required |
|-------|-------------|:--------:|
| Title | Model name (max 200 chars.) | Yes |
| Type | Experience, Tool or Approach | Yes |
| Complexity | Beginner, Intermediate or Advanced | No |
| Associated approach | Link to an existing approach (tools and experiences) | No |
| Description | Model overview (max 5000 chars.) | Yes |
| Sections | Depend on the chosen type | No |
| Tags | Keywords separated by commas | No |

### Publication workflow

1. You submit your model -> status **Pending validation**
2. An administrator validates -> the model becomes a **Draft**
3. You progress the model: Draft -> Under review -> Testing -> **Published**

## 7. Contributors

The **Contributors** page highlights the practitioners who bring the Lab to life. Accessible from the navigation menu.

### Contributor list

Displays all users with at least one model or post, sorted by activity. Each card shows:
- Thumbnail photo and name
- Short bio
- Expertise tags (first 4 displayed)
- Number of models and posts

### Detailed profile

By clicking on a contributor, you access their full page with:
- **Background and skills** — long-form CV in Markdown describing their journey, training and experience
- **Personal links** — website, LinkedIn, YouTube channel, training page...
- Published **models**
- **Contributions** to other authors' models
- Forum **posts**

Each contributor can enrich their profile from the **Profile** page (name, bio, CV, expertise, links).

## 8. Community

The community forum allows members to share discussions, questions and feedback from experience.

### Categories

| Category | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| General | Open discussions |
| Models | Discussions about NLP models |
| Experiences | Field experience reports |
| Questions | Questions for the community |
| Resources | Sharing useful resources |

### Create a post

1. Click **New post** (requires being signed in)
2. Fill in the title, content, category and optionally a linked model
3. Click **Publish**

### Interact

- **Like** a post: click the heart
- **Comment**: click the comment icon
- **Filter**: click a category
- **Edit**: the author can edit their post via the pencil icon
- **Delete**: the author can delete their post via the trash icon

## 11. Profile

### My profile

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Avatar** | Click the camera icon to upload an image (max 2 MB) |
| **Display name** | Your name on the platform |
| **Short bio** | A sentence or two about yourself (max 500 chars.) |
| **Background and skills** | Long-form CV describing your journey, training, experience (max 5000 chars., Markdown supported) |
| **Areas of expertise** | Skill tags (type + Enter to add) |
| **Personal links** | Website, LinkedIn, YouTube, training page... (label + URL) |

A **"View public profile"** link at the top of the page lets you preview how it looks.

### Public profile

By clicking on an **author's name** (on a model sheet, in the library or in the forum), you access their public profile which displays:
- Their avatar, name, bio and personal links
- Their background and skills (CV in Markdown)
- Registration date
- Their published **models**
- Models where they are listed as a **contributor** in the evolution journal
- Their forum **posts**
